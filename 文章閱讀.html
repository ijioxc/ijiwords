S 8.8
They Call Me "Bob" — 美國社交習慣
A guide to American customs: names, greetings, body language, and punctuality.
🎧
English Original
中文翻譯
OK, here are some things that I think would be useful if you're visiting the States, especially if you're coming here to do business.
So, first of all, with names. When you first meet someone, it's considered good manners to use a title along with their last name, you know, like Mr. Smith or Ms. Jones, and so on. But the funny thing is that, in fact, people will in most cases want to use first names — like they'll say, "Hey, call me Bob," you know? If you stick to a more formal "Mr. Smith" sort of thing, you'll find most people will periodically use your name in a conversation. If you're not used to it, it can seem a little strange.
Now, with meeting people for the first time, it's pretty normal for people to shake hands, certainly in business. Americans tend to avoid greetings that involve being too close — hugging, kissing — with people they don't know well. With family members and friends, however, don't be surprised if someone gives you a hug or a kiss on the cheek. You sort of have to keep your eyes open and try to anticipate what they'll do.
Where applicable, it's also quite normal for people to cross their legs with one ankle on the other knee. I know this is rude in some countries, but it's quite normal here. Also, when meeting someone in an office, don't be surprised if they sit on their desk — that's a big no-no in places like Japan, but here it's fine.
To show agreement, they'll sometimes give the thumbs up, or they'll make a circle out of their thumb and index finger — that means "OK" in the United States.
Also, don't be offended if someone looks you directly in the eye. In America, maintaining eye contact is expected of you.
Last of all, timing. Punctuality is very important in the United States. If you are going to be late, you need to let people know — it's the same for social occasions. You need to arrive on time.
Of course, having said all this, it's important to remember that the United States is huge and there's a lot of variety in what's acceptable in different places. Anyway, I hope this advice helps.
好，以下是一些對你造訪美國很有幫助的事情，尤其是如果你是來這裡做生意的。
首先是稱呼。第一次見到某人時，使用頭銜加上姓氏被認為是有禮貌的，例如「史密斯先生」或「瓊斯女士」等等。但有趣的是，事實上大多數情況下人們都會想要直接叫名字——比如他們會說「叫我鮑勃就好」。如果你堅持用比較正式的「史密斯先生」，你會發現大多數人在對話中會不時叫你的名字。如果你不習慣，可能會覺得有點奇怪。
至於第一次見面的打招呼方式，握手是相當普遍的，在商業場合尤其如此。美國人傾向於避免與不熟識的人有過於親密的問候方式，例如擁抱或親吻臉頰。不過，對於家人或朋友，如果有人給你擁抱或親吻臉頰，不要覺得驚訝。你需要注意觀察並揣摩對方的習慣。
在適用的情況下，美國人也常常翹二郎腿——把腳踝放在另一條腿的膝蓋上。我知道這在某些國家是不禮貌的，但在美國是很普通的動作。另外，在辦公室跟人見面時，對方可能會坐在桌子上——這在日本是大忌，但在美國很正常。
表示同意時，他們有時會豎起大拇指，或用拇指和食指圈成一個圓圈——在美國這個手勢代表「OK」。
另外，如果有人直視你的眼睛，不要覺得被冒犯。在美國，保持眼神接觸是對你的期待，代表你誠懇專注。
最後是時間觀念。守時在美國非常重要。如果你要遲到，你需要通知對方——社交場合也是一樣，要準時到達。
當然，說了這些之後，要記住美國非常大，不同地方的習俗可能差異很大。總之，希望這些建議對你有所幫助。

S 9.8
Change the Things You Can
An article about positive attitude: believing bad things pass, thinking rationally, and focusing on what you can control.
🎧
English Original
中文翻譯
We all have problems in our lives, but what makes a real difference is how we react to them. Psychologists say that people with a positive attitude have three key traits.
The first is that they believe that bad things don't last forever. Most negative situations are temporary, and the people who cope best are those who keep reminding themselves of that.
The second is that they think rationally — in other words, they use their head rather than their heart. In some situations this can be difficult. Here's an example from my own experience. I knew that a colleague of mine was going to be promoted, but I had found out before the official announcement. A friend suggested I should use the information to make an impression on some people I knew. But I decided not to. I thought it was wrong to use information that wasn't officially mine. I'm sure that in the end, it worked in my favor — I was promoted the following year.
The third — and probably the most important — is that they distinguish between what they can change and what they cannot. There are things in life that we have absolutely no control over. Worrying about them is a total waste of time and energy. Instead, people with a positive attitude focus their attention on the things they can actually do something about.
So, next time you're feeling stressed about something, ask yourself: "Can I change this?" If the answer is yes — do something. If the answer is no — stop worrying and move on.
我們每個人的生活中都有問題，但真正造成差異的是我們如何回應這些問題。心理學家說，擁有積極態度的人有三個關鍵特質。
第一是他們相信壞事不會永遠持續。大多數負面情況都是暫時的，而那些應對得最好的人，就是不斷提醒自己這一點的人。
第二是他們用理性思考——換句話說，他們用頭腦而非情感來做決定。在某些情況下，這可能很難做到。這裡舉一個我自身的例子。我知道一位同事即將被升遷，但我是在官方公告之前就得知這個消息的。一位朋友建議我利用這個消息在某些認識的人面前留下好印象。但我決定不這樣做。我認為利用不屬於我的消息是不對的。我確信最終這對我產生了正面影響——我在隔年被晉升了。
第三——也可能是最重要的——是他們能夠區分哪些事情是他們可以改變的，哪些是他們無法改變的。生活中有些事情我們完全無法掌控，為這些事情擔心是完全浪費時間和精力的。相反地，擁有積極態度的人會把注意力集中在他們真的能夠採取行動的事情上。
所以，下次當你對某件事感到壓力時，問問自己：「我能改變這件事嗎？」如果答案是「是的」——就去做些什麼。如果答案是「不能」——就停止擔心，繼續前進。

🎧
English Original
中文翻譯
Midnight in Paris is set in Paris in the present and in different periods in the past. It stars Owen Wilson as Gil, a Hollywood screenwriter, and Rachel McAdams as Inez, his fiancée.
As the movie opens, Gil is on holiday in Paris with Inez and her wealthy parents. He is supposed to be in love with his girlfriend, but his love affair really seems to be with Paris in the springtime. One evening, while wandering around the city, he gets lost, and, as the clock strikes midnight, an old Peugeot car pulls up. Inside the car are a group of party-goers dressed in 1920s costumes, who invite Gil to join them. He is transported to a party where he meets some of his intellectual and artistic heroes from that time, and falls in love with Adriana, Picasso's mistress.
Meanwhile, in the present, Gil's bride-to-be and her parents become more and more annoyed and suspicious about his nightly disappearances. Eventually Gil realizes he's not going to live in his beloved Paris, breaks up with Inez, and gets her to leave.
Directed by Woody Allen, the movie cuts between the glitter of Paris in the twenties and the present-day city. The script is alternately gripping, shocking, and hilarious, and the camerawork is stunning. As Gil, Owen Wilson is appealing in his enthusiasm and love of Paris in the past. However, for me, it is the character of Adriana, convincingly acted by Marion Cotillard, who is the most fascinating of all.
The movie follows sparkling wit and engaging leaps forward and backward in time. Midnight in Paris is a light, delightful movie that I'd thoroughly recommend.
